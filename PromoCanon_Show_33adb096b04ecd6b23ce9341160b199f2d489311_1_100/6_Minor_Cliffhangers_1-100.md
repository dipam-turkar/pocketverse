### Strategic Function:

Minor cliffhangers are the narrative's connective tissue, strategically deployed to maintain reader engagement between the major, arc-altering revelations. They function primarily as scene-setters and short-term tension builders, posing immediate questions ("Will she be humiliated?" "Will he be discovered?") that are typically resolved within the first few scenes of the following episode. This provides a satisfying rhythm of tension and quick release, preventing the "cliffhanger fatigue" that can result from constant high-stakes threats. They also serve to flesh out the world and its secondary characters, using contained conflicts—like Angela's bullying, Rachel's social scheming, or Cherry's kindergarten power struggles—to explore the story's central themes of status, identity, and hidden strength on a smaller, more personal scale.
